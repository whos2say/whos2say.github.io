About the Foundation

The Who's to Say ? Foundation is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit (33-3826585) based in Ocean County, NJ. We challenge low expectations by building spaces where creative young adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities can thrive—through community, practice, and purpose.

Inclusion

Everyone belongs. We design programs that honor unique interests, access needs, and communication styles.

Independence

We coach real-world skills—creative, social, and practical—so participants grow confidence inside and outside the studio.

Remix Mindset

We upcycle tools, stories, and identities. Participants remix culture to create original work and discover their voice.

The Remix Studio & Beyond

Our starter track supports skill-building, career exploration, and daily independence. These offerings connect directly to our six Possibility Paths on the Programs page.

Remix Studio

Digital Storytelling

Small-group and 1:1 coaching in podcasting, video, streaming, and content creation using accessible tools (CapCut, OBS, Canva). Sessions center on personal interests like gaming, music, and sports, building communication and self-advocacy through making.

Typical cadence: 2× per week · 2 hours · 3-month pilot with monthly reviews.

Micro-enterprise

Supported Creative Business

A personalized creative business incubator. Explore and launch a simple micro-enterprise (e.g., content channel, digital art, custom merch) using tools like Canva and Printify. Coaching covers branding, marketing, budgeting, and goal-setting.

Typical cadence: 1× per week · ~3 hours · 3-month pilot with extension options.

Daily tech

Technology for Independence

1:1 coaching to use everyday tech—calendar apps, reminders, transportation, visual schedules, and communication tools—to support time management, routines, and safer community navigation. Family/staff training available.

Typical cadence: 1× per week · ~90 minutes · Ongoing with quarterly updates.

Donate & Partner

We’re raising foundational capital to launch the Remix Studio and lay the groundwork for a future live/work independent living residence for neurodiverse young adults.

What your support fuels

  • Founding team capacity — time to build, pilot, and refine programs.
  • Program development & staffing for the Remix Studio and community-based work.
  • Studio space — lease or real estate securing for a creative home base.
  • Technology & equipment — cameras, mics, PCs/Macs, adaptive setups.
  • Planning for a future live/work independent-living residence.

Ways to partner now

  • Individual gifts: one-time or recurring support.
  • Corporate partners: sponsor a cohort, equip a station, or match employee giving.
  • In-kind: hardware, software licenses, creative tools, and services.
