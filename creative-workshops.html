Public Creative Workshops

Creative media, storytelling, and maker experiences for people of all ages and abilities.

Who’s to Say? offers public creative workshops, private creative coaching, and project-based studio sessions where participants can explore photography, video, music, gaming, design, sports media, storytelling, legacy projects, and hands-on creative builds.

Our sessions are interest-driven, welcoming, and built around real creative work — helping each person build confidence, communication, technical skills, connection, and a stronger sense of what is possible.

Explore Workshops Contact Us

Creative opportunities open to the community.

Who’s to Say? is building a creative studio model that welcomes people of all ages and abilities. Our workshops and private sessions are available to the general public and can be adapted around each participant’s interests, experience level, communication style, and creative goals.

Some people come to learn a specific tool. Some come to build confidence. Some want to tell their story, create content, explore music, document sports, preserve family memories, design something original, or simply try a new creative experience in a supportive environment.

We are especially interested in creative projects that bring generations together — helping older adults preserve stories, photos, music, and memories while giving younger creators and participants meaningful roles in capturing, designing, editing, and sharing those stories.

The common thread is simple: creativity without comparison.

Workshop and studio session options

Sessions may be offered as private creative coaching, small-group workshops, family-supported projects, intergenerational storytelling sessions, community workshops, or customized studio blocks depending on the participant’s goals and the type of creative work involved.

Storytelling

Plan, record, edit, and share personal stories through video, photos, captions, voice, and simple publishing workflows.

Inclusive and intergenerational by design

Who’s to Say? was built around the belief that creativity should not be limited by comparison, labels, age, confidence, or low expectations. We welcome people with different learning styles, communication styles, support needs, life experiences, and creative backgrounds.

Our approach is flexible, patient, practical, and project-based. We focus on helping each person participate meaningfully, make choices, use real tools, and experience the pride of creating something that reflects who they are.

We also believe communities are stronger when people of different generations have a place to create together. Seniors may bring stories, photos, music, memories, and local history. Younger creators and participants may bring energy, curiosity, technical tools, and new ways to share. The studio becomes the bridge.

Using approved funding sources

Families using self-directed budgets, third-party funding, grants, scholarships, or other approved funding sources may be able to use those funds for Who’s to Say? workshops or private creative sessions when the service is approved through the individual’s plan or funding source.

We can provide general descriptions, session summaries, invoices, and supporting documentation upon request. Approval decisions are made by the applicable funding source, plan, or reviewer.

Information for Support Coordinators

Building the next phase: a community creative space.

Today, Who’s to Say? offers creative workshops, private coaching, intergenerational storytelling, and project-based studio sessions. Our next phase is to grow this work into a community-centered digital media and maker space — a welcoming place where people of different ages, abilities, backgrounds, and life experiences can create, collaborate, learn modern tools, and share their stories.

This future space will support digital storytelling, photography, video, music, gaming, design, maker projects, community exhibits, sports media, creator workshops, family legacy projects, photo digitizing, accessible technology experiences, and intergenerational community memory projects.

Funds raised through donations, sponsorships, grants, memberships, and community partnerships will help us expand access to equipment, studio space, adaptive tools, creative technology, workshops, scholarships, and community programming.
