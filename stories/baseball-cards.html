A Who’s to Say ? Media Team Case Study

Turning players into heroes.

How young adult creators on the Who’s to Say Media Team used photography, design, and storytelling to turn a group of adaptive baseball players into trading-card heroes — and gave their families a keepsake to treasure.

See what support makes possible Read the story →

The overview

A team’s first season, remembered forever.

The Point Pleasant Panthers Challenger Baseball program played its very first season — and the Who’s to Say Media Team was there for it. We came to document practices and Opening Day, then had an idea: what if every player got their own custom trading card?

Working with accessible design tools, young adult creators from the Who’s to Say Media Team captured photos, gathered each player’s personality and dreams, and turned them into personalized, trading-card-style keepsakes. The result was a hands-on media production experience — and a gift these families will keep.

The opportunity

Every athlete deserves to be celebrated.

Younger adaptive athletes deserve the same excitement, creativity, and pride as any other player. Families often have photos from the season — but rarely a polished, personalized keepsake that captures who their child really is.

Not just a face in a team picture, but the player’s spirit: their nickname, their favorite food, their dream job, the spirit animal they’d pick, the superpower they wish they had.

That gap was the opportunity. What if the players became the heroes — and young creators with disabilities were the ones who made it happen?

The Who’s to Say Media Team role

Not just participants. Creators.

Young adults with disabilities didn’t watch this project happen — they ran it. The Media Team brought the cards to life, stepping into real creative roles at every stage of production.

  1. 01

    Photographer

    Captured player portraits and candid team moments at practices and on Opening Day.

  2. 02

    Interviewer

    Sat with each player to learn their favorites, dreams, strengths, and personality.

  3. 03

    Designer

    Learned how templates, layouts, and design layers come together into a card.

  4. 04

    Editor

    Selected and refined images, adjusted backgrounds, and cleaned up each layer.

  5. 05

    Storyteller

    Shaped each player’s details into a short, joyful, respectful card story.

  6. 06

    Creative Director

    Made the calls on look and feel — reviewing choices so every card felt right.

  7. 07

    Production Assistant

    Organized files, tracked versions, and helped move each card to the finish line.

  8. Who’s to Say Sports Media

The creative process

From player photos to hero cards.

A clear, repeatable workflow — simple enough to learn, real enough to feel like professional media production.

  1. Capture

    Photograph each player and the team moments that tell the season’s story.

  2. Listen

    Interview each player about their personality, favorites, dreams, and strengths.

  3. Organize

    Gather every detail into a creative storytelling template, one player at a time.

  4. Design

    Build the card with design layers, image editing, and trading-card layouts.

  5. Refine

    Review and revise so each card feels personal, joyful, and respectful.

  6. Deliver

    Produce a finished keepsake families and players can hold, share, and treasure.

Why it matters

Who’s to Say creates space for people with disabilities to be both the subject and the author of meaningful stories.

This project proves the model in miniature: accessible creative technology, inclusive teams and real roles, and trust. Give young creators the tools and the purpose, and they make work that celebrates others, builds confidence, and strengthens community.
