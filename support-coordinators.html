Support Coordinator Information

Creative workshop descriptions and documentation for plan review.

Who’s to Say? offers public creative workshops, private creative coaching, intergenerational storytelling, and project-based studio sessions focused on creative media, storytelling, digital tools, communication, confidence, and community participation.

This page provides general language that families and Support Coordinators may use when considering whether a workshop or session may be appropriate for an individual’s plan or funding source.

View Creative Workshops Contact Us

What Who’s to Say? provides

Who’s to Say? provides public creative media workshops and project-based studio sessions that can be individualized around a participant’s interests, goals, preferred tools, and communication style.

Sessions may include activities such as photography, video creation, podcast-style recording, music and playlist storytelling, sports media, gaming content, graphic design, creator tools, legacy storytelling, photo digitizing, community memory projects, and hands-on maker projects.

The focus is on meaningful creative participation, communication, confidence, digital skill-building, project completion, and community connection.

Sample description language

Families and Support Coordinators may adapt the following language when describing a session request or creative workshop goal.

General creative workshop description

Public creative media workshop or private creative coaching session focused on digital storytelling, creative skill-building, communication, confidence, and project-based participation through photography, video, music, design, gaming, sports media, legacy storytelling, or maker activities.

Project-based studio description

Project-based creative studio session designed around the participant’s interests and goals, supporting planning, creative decision-making, use of digital tools, communication, and completion of a tangible media or maker project.

Community-centered creative participation

Inclusive creative workshop experience that supports participation in community-based media, storytelling, design, photography, audio, video, or maker projects while building confidence, communication, and creative independence.

Intergenerational storytelling description

Creative storytelling session focused on preserving stories, photos, music, memories, interviews, or community history through digital media, captions, photo organization, audio, video, or simple design projects.

Sample invoice language

When requested, invoices may describe the specific public workshop or creative session provided, including the date, session type, rate, length, and project focus.

  • Creative media workshop / private creative coaching session focused on project-based digital storytelling, communication, creative skill-building, and use of media tools aligned with the participant’s approved goals and interests.
  • Project-based studio session focused on photography, video, music, design, sports media, maker activities, or legacy storytelling, including planning, creative decision-making, use of digital tools, and completion of a media or creative project.

Invoice language can be adjusted to accurately reflect the actual session provided, the approved plan language, and the documentation needs of the family or funding source.

Documentation available upon request

Who’s to Say? can provide basic documentation to support review and recordkeeping, including:

  • public workshop or session descriptions
  • invoices with date, rate, session type, and project focus
  • brief session summaries
  • examples of creative activities offered
  • general rate information
  • contact information for follow-up questions

Approval decisions are made by the applicable funding source, plan, or reviewer. We are happy to provide accurate descriptions of the service offered and the creative work completed.

Public access and funding review

Who’s to Say? workshops and private creative sessions are public-facing creative opportunities. They are designed to be inclusive and accessible while remaining available to the broader community.

Families using self-directed budgets, third-party funding, grants, scholarships, or other approved funding sources may be able to use those funds when the workshop or session is approved through the individual’s plan or funding source.

A growing community creative space

Who’s to Say? is also raising funds for the next phase of this work: a community-centered digital media and maker space where people of different ages, abilities, backgrounds, and life experiences can create, collaborate, learn modern tools, and share their stories.

This vision supports a broader public benefit — expanding access to creative technology, media tools, workshops, studio experiences, intergenerational storytelling, digital literacy, family legacy projects, photo preservation, community memory work, and accessible creative tools.
