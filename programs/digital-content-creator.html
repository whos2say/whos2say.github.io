Purpose

Digital Content Creator Pathways helps young adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities build confidence and real-world skills through digital media, design, and technology.

This pathway is not about one-size-fits-all job training. Instead, we focus on discovering each person’s interests and translating them into meaningful, practical skills that can grow over time.

How It Works

Supports are delivered through individualized or small-group coaching sessions. Participants work on projects that matter to them, such as editing a gaming clip, designing a logo, recording a podcast, or creating short videos to share.

Core Areas of Learning

Video & Content Creation

  • Recording video using phones or basic cameras
  • Editing with accessible tools (CapCut, iMovie, etc.)
  • Adding titles, captions, and simple effects
  • Exporting and safely sharing finished videos

Digital Art & Design

  • Creating graphics in Canva or similar tools
  • Designing thumbnails, posters, and social images
  • Learning basic visual branding concepts

Photography & Media Basics

  • Using a smartphone or entry-level camera
  • Framing, lighting, and simple editing
  • Building a small personal photo portfolio

Studio & Production Skills

  • Basics of microphones, lighting, and camera setup
  • On-camera vs. behind-the-camera roles
  • Working as part of a creative crew

Outcomes & Skills

  • Increased confidence with technology and media tools
  • Improved communication, planning, and follow-through
  • A small digital portfolio to share with families and coordinators
  • Experience working toward goals and deadlines
