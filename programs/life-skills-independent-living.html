Life Skills & Independent Living Preparation

Everyday skills for confidence and independence—routines, home tasks, money basics, and community navigation.

Routines Home Skills Money Basics Navigation
Best for: Young adults preparing for more independence at home and in the community.

Purpose

Life Skills & Independent Living Preparation focuses on the everyday habits and routines that make independence possible. It’s not about doing everything alone; it’s about building skills, confidence, and choices.

Core Life Skill Areas

Daily Routines

  • Using calendars, alarms, or visual schedules

Home & Kitchen Skills

  • Simple meal prep and kitchen safety
  • Cleaning up and organizing
  • Using checklists to complete tasks

Money & Shopping

  • Handling cash and cards in real or practice scenarios
  • Comparing prices, making simple budgets
  • Practicing purchases in real stores when appropriate

Community Navigation

  • Using rides with family, paratransit, or rideshare safely
  • Learning routes to familiar places (library, rec center, etc.)

Social & Emotional Skills

  • Practicing conversation and self-advocacy
  • Managing frustration and asking for help
  • Building positive self-talk and confidence

Outcomes & Skills

  • More predictable days and routines
  • Increased participation in home tasks
  • Improved comfort in stores and community spaces
  • Stronger communication and self-advocacy

Session Style

  • Recurring, practical coaching sessions
  • Goal-driven supports tied to daily life
  • Community practice when appropriate

How It Works

Using DDD Goods & Services, families can schedule recurring sessions where a support professional works alongside the participant on practical, goal-driven skills.

Coordinator / DDD Summary

Life Skills & Independent Living sessions are individualized supports funded through DDD Goods & Services. Focus areas include daily routines, simple meal preparation, basic money skills, and community navigation. Supports are practical, goal-driven, and designed to increase the person’s independence and participation at home and in the community.
