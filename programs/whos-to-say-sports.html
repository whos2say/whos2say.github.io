Who’s to Say Sports

Challenger Sports, fitness, outings, and sports storytelling—built around each participant’s interests.

Participation Media Crew Outings Confidence
Best for: Participants who love sports and want to play, cheer, talk about the game, or be part of a media crew.

Purpose

Who’s to Say Sports blends athletics, sports media, and community engagement. It’s for participants who love sports—whether they want to play, volunteer, talk about the game, or help create coverage as part of a “media crew.”

Program Elements

Sports Participation & Wellness

  • Joining Challenger or rec teams (with support if needed)
  • Practicing skills, teamwork, and sportsmanship
  • Fitness activities that are fun and accessible

Sports Media & Commentary

  • Recording short game recaps or “fan takes”
  • Interviewing teammates, coaches, or friends
  • Learning how to film highlights on a phone
  • Participating in simple “Who’s to Say Sports” podcast segments

Community & Events

  • Group outings to games
  • Spirit nights or watch parties
  • Opportunities to meet teams or visit fields/arenas

Outcomes & Skills

  • Social skills, teamwork, and confidence
  • Better understanding of health and movement
  • Communication skills through interviews and commentary
  • Stronger sense of belonging and community pride

Session Style

  • Community-based outings
  • Team participation + skill building
  • Sports media projects

How It Works

In colaboration with local HS sports teams and community volunteers, we help expand the reach of existing programs and help create new ones.

  • Transportation and support to participate in Challenger Sports or inclusive leagues
  • Outings to local high school, college, or pro games
  • Sessions focused on filming, interviewing, and editing simple sports content
Coordinator / DDD Summary

Supports funded via DDD Goods & Services may include: assisting the person to participate in Challenger or other inclusive sports, structured group fitness activities, and supervised outings to games. Sessions can also focus on sports media skills—filming, commentary, and interviews—to build communication and community engagement skills.
