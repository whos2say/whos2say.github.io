Art Studio & Events

Create, share, and celebrate work in supportive settings—confidence and pride first.

Create Present Confidence Value
Best for: Creators who light up when people see and appreciate what they make.

Purpose

Art Studio & Events is about creating, sharing and entrepreneurship. Participants will build their personal brands by designing and making things like; digital art, photography, crafts, 3D prints and more. Then they practice presenting them at events or markets. It’s about confidence, pride, and safe exposure to micro-enterprise concepts.

Core Areas of Learning

Creation Sessions

  • Design sessions (digital or physical)
  • Simple products: stickers, prints, keychains, small crafts
  • Building sets of items for a future display table or online gallery

Presentation & Event Practice

  • Learning how to set up a table or display
  • Practicing how to talk about their work
  • Participating in low-pressure showcases or markets (when available)

Money & Value Concepts

  • Basic ideas of pricing and value (age-appropriate and person-centered)
  • Understanding that their creativity has worth
  • When appropriate, exploring simple ways items could be gifted, raffled, or sold under family/agency guidance

Outcomes & Skills

  • Pride in completed creative work
  • Improved communication and presentation skills
  • Early exposure to entrepreneurship concepts
  • Positive public experiences and recognition

Session Style

  • Project-based creation sessions
  • Presentation practice + confidence-building
  • Optional event participation

How It Works

Participants create art, prints, and small projects, then practice sharing them at events and showcases — building confidence, communication skills, and pride in their work.

Coordinator / DDD Summary

Art Studio & Events uses Goods & Services-funded sessions to support the person in designing and creating items (digital or physical) and, where appropriate, participating in small showcases or events. Emphasis is on skill development, social interaction, and self-esteem rather than commercial sales.
