Community Integration & Self-Advocacy

Showing up, speaking up, and being seen.

Image Placeholder

Community events, outings, or advocacy storytelling

Purpose

This program helps participants show up, speak up, and be seen in their community. It blends supported outings with creative advocacy and storytelling.

How It Works

Supports are delivered through community-based sessions where a staff member accompanies the participant to events and/or helps them create advocacy content afterward.

Core Components

Community Outings

  • Local events, fairs, concerts, and art shows
  • Library visits, workshops, clubs, or meetups
  • Volunteering or observing volunteer roles

Advocacy & Storytelling

  • Helping participants tell their story through video, audio, or art
  • Creating positive digital content with family approval
  • Practicing speaking up about needs and preferences

Relationship-Building

  • Meeting community partners and local leaders
  • Practicing introductions and conversations
  • Finding places participants want to return to regularly

Outcomes & Skills

  • Increased comfort in community spaces
  • Stronger self-advocacy and personal voice
  • Real connections with local people and organizations
  • Positive representation of people with disabilities

Coordinator / DDD Summary

Goods & Services-funded supports focus on community engagement, social skills, and advocacy through supported outings and storytelling projects aligned with the person’s interests.
