Outings Advocacy Storytelling Relationships

Purpose

This program helps participants show up, speak up, and be seen in their community. It combines supported outings with creative advocacy and storytelling.

How It Works

Supports are scheduled as community-based sessions where a staff member accompanies the participant to events and/or helps them create advocacy content afterward.

Core Components

Community Outings

  • Local events, fairs, concerts, art shows
  • Library visits, workshops, clubs, or meetups
  • Volunteering or observing volunteer roles

Advocacy & Storytelling

  • Helping participants tell their story in their own way (video, audio, writing, art)
  • Creating positive social media or digital content (with family approval)
  • Practicing speaking up about their needs and preferences

Relationship-Building

  • Meeting community partners (coaches, artists, librarians, business owners)
  • Practicing introductions and conversations
  • Finding places they want to return to regularly

Outcomes & Skills

  • Increased comfort in community spaces
  • Stronger self-advocacy and voice
  • Real connections with local people and organizations
  • Positive representation of people with disabilities in the community

Coordinator / DDD Summary

Supports funded through DDD Goods & Services focus on helping the person engage in community activities they care about and express themselves through advocacy. This may include attending events with support, practicing social skills, and working on simple advocacy or storytelling projects (video, writing, or art) that reflect the personâ€™s interests and goals.

Best For

Self-advocates and anyone who wants to be more active in their community.

Session Style

  • Community-based sessions
  • Supported practice + reflection
  • Creative storytelling follow-up
